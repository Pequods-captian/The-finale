Understanding the CIA traid and authentication

Computer & Information Science - Seaway Tech

Explore the core principles every cybersecurity professional must know — Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability, and how we prove who we are in a digital world.

Confidentiality

Protection against unauthorized access. Only those with proper permission should ever be able to read or view the data. Think of it as a locked diary — only the owner (or those granted access) can read it

Real world breach Edward Snowden's 2013 leak of classified NSA government data is a landmark example of a Confidentiality breach.

Integrity

Protection against unauthorized modification. Data must remain accurate and trustworthy from the moment it is created until it is accessed. If someone changes your grade in the grade book without permission, that's an Integrity breach.

Real world breach The Stuxnet worm secretly altered code on Iranian centrifuge machines, causing them to spin too fast and physically break — a classic Integrity attack.

Availability

Protection against denial of service. Authorized users must be able to access data and systems whenever they legitimately need them. A website that is always down, or an account that stays locked, fails the Availability test.

REAL-WORLD BREACH The Mirai Botnet used 145,000+ infected cameras and devices to flood security journalist Brian Krebs' website with traffic, crashing it for 4 days — a textbook DDoS / Availability breach.
